I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Alliance for Social, Political, Ethical, and Cultural Thought (ASPECT) at Virginia Tech.

Carceral Studies & Decolonial Theory: My research focuses on the "carceral digital turn" and the evolution of prisoner resistance. I utilize the American Prison Writing Archive (APWA) to center the lived experiences and "shadow canons" of incarcerated intellectuals, challenging state-sanctioned narratives of technological control.

Critical Sociology & Social Thought: I investigate the intersection of racial capitalism, surveillance, and epistemic authority. Drawing on Stuart Hall, Frantz Fanon, and Karl Marx, I theorize how carceral technologies—from electronic monitoring to digital communication platforms—reproduce colonial hierarchies and how these systems are contested from within.
